Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is a technique used in AI to improve responses by combining a language model with external knowledge sources. Instead of relying only on what the model was trained on, RAG retrieves relevant information from documents or databases and uses it to generate better answers. This helps make responses more accurate, up-to-date, and context-aware. Typically, documents are split into smaller chunks, converted into embeddings, and stored in a vector database. When a user asks a question, the system retrieves the most relevant chunks and passes them to the model to generate a final response.
